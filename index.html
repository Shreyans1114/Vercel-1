The Advisory

What FSSAI's advisory actually says
and what it doesn't

MINISTRY OF AYUSH ADVISORY, 2021 · ENFORCED BY FSSAI SCHEDULE IV, APRIL 2026

Ashwagandha leaves in crude or extract or any other form is not permitted.

What is FSSAI?
The Food Safety and Standards Authority of India India's apex food regulatory body under the Ministry of Health. Schedule IV governs nutraceuticals and health supplements.
What is Ministry of AYUSH?
The Ministry of Ayurveda, Yoga & Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha and Homoeopathy. Its 2021 advisory confirmed that only root is endorsed for internal therapeutic use.

Rule 01
Only root is permitted
FSSAI Schedule IV has always allowed only dried mature root of Withania somnifera in nutraceuticals. The April 2026 advisory is enforcement of an existing rule not new legislation.
Rule 02
Leaf was never permitted
Ashwagandha leaf was never a permitted ingredient under Schedule IV - the April 2026 advisory enforces a rule that always existed. State authorities have been directed to take legal action against non-compliant products.
Rule 03
A label claim is not protection
Brands with a 'root only' label but without independent documentation may be exposed if there is an independent assessment. The label and the Certificate of Analysis must agree. Regulators check both.
The Science

The root and the leaf have different bioactive compositions. Modern science is only validating what Ayurveda always knew.

🌿 3,000+ years - Ayurvedic texts have specified root only for internal therapeutic use. The Ayurvedic Pharmacopoeia of India (API, Part I, Vol. I) confirms it.

The beneficial withanolides that your formulation depends on for its efficacy claims are concentrated in the root. The leaf carries a fundamentally different phytochemical profile.

Ask your supplier for a toxicity profile on their ingredient. If they cannot provide one, that is your answer.

Your Brand

Four things worth checking
for your brand

Not a compliance checklist. Four practical questions a brand professional should be asking their QA and procurement teams.

Supply Chain
Does your Certificate of Analysis explicitly state 'Withania somnifera root' as the botanical source? A supplier's verbal assurance is not independent verification.
→ Ask your QA team: have we reviewed the CoA?
Label Compliance
Do your labels, e-commerce listings, and website accurately describe the plant part? A mismatch between product and label is independently actionable.
→ Check: does every channel say 'root extract'?
Withanolide Claims
Does your withanolide percentage use USP profile by HPLC? A total % may include compounds from not-withanolide material.
→ Ask: is this a USP profile or a generic total?
Science and Dose
Does the dose used in the clinical studies match the dose on your label? A study conducted at a different dose does not validate the efficacy if a different daily dose is recommended on label.
  • Is your Ingredient supported by a published Pharmokinetic Profile?

    • Study of pharmacokinetics is a crucial step in translational research and helps in determination of optimal dose and dosing frequency for getting the best efficacy results.
